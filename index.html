A Wanderer's Ballad
Song / Prose poem · English
In the quiet moments, when the world hushed to a whisper, I couldn't help but reflect on the words spoken to me long ago. Destiny, they said, was my eternal companion, my guiding star. It was a promise of new lands, exotic tastes, and an insatiable love for adventure that coursed through my veins. There's something intoxicating about the anonymity of a foreign city, like the empty pages of a journal yearning to be filled with ink. Under a foreign sky, I could rewrite my beginnings and craft new endings, each step a revelation of self. No plans, no fixed destinations, no expectations; they warned me that I'd never be content with less. But what they didn't tell me was whether I'd be fortunate enough to embark on this adventure with another soul, a kindred spirit seeking the same uncharted territories of the heart. We'd start in the heart of India, the vibrant and chaotic streets of Delhi... "Come with me to Greece," I'd whisper, feeling the cool breeze off Santorini's white stairs... Home, you'd tell me, wasn't a fixed location but a feeling nestled deep within us, within each other. You, my love, would be the walking journal I'd always needed, reciting how my words danced in your soul, reflected my passion, and understood my pain. A living testament that I had lived and loved, for you were, and forever would be, my grandest adventure.
